🥚 Eggs

How much do your chicken eggs cost?

Chicken eggs are $7 per dozen. We accept cash, Venmo, Zelle, and other digital payment options.

Are your eggs washed?

Yes. Our eggs are washed and refrigerated immediately after collection for food safety. We use Carefree Enzymes egg wash — a food-safe cleaner we chose ourselves (not sponsored). Once washed, eggs must be kept refrigerated.

How do I care for my eggs after I bring them home?

Keep them refrigerated at all times. Because we wash our eggs before selling, the protective bloom is removed — meaning they must stay cold, just like store-bought eggs. Store them pointy-end down in the original carton to preserve freshness longest.

How long will my eggs stay fresh?

Properly refrigerated, our washed eggs stay fresh for 4–5 weeks from the collection date. Farm-fresh eggs often outlast store eggs because they haven't sat in distribution for weeks before reaching you.

How do I check for freshness?

Use the float test: fill a bowl with cold water and gently place the egg in it. A fresh egg sinks and lays flat. An older egg stands upright. A floating egg should be discarded. You can also crack it onto a plate — a fresh egg has a firm, rounded yolk and thick white.

Do I need to wash the eggs before using them?

No — we've already washed them before packaging. There's no need to re-wash. In fact, washing right before use and then not cooking immediately can introduce bacteria, so it's best to wash only if needed and use right away.

Why won't my hard-boiled eggs peel properly?

This is the most common complaint about farm-fresh eggs — and it's actually a sign of quality. Very fresh eggs cling to their shell membrane. The fix: use eggs that are 1–2 weeks old for hard boiling, add a teaspoon of baking soda to the water, and immediately transfer to an ice bath after cooking. Older eggs (closer to 3 weeks) peel much more easily.

Is there anything different about cooking with farm-fresh eggs?

Yes — in the best way. Farm-fresh eggs have richer, more orange yolks and firmer whites due to the hens' pasture diet and non-GMO feed. You may notice they behave slightly differently in baked goods (richer, more flavorful) and that fried or poached eggs hold their shape better. Reduce added fat slightly in recipes if needed since the yolks are naturally richer.

What colors of eggs do you sell?

Our 60 hens lay a beautiful variety: brown, cream, white, blue, green, chocolate, and speckled. The color depends on the breed — it doesn't affect taste or nutrition. A carton from Ohana often includes a mix of colors, which many customers love.

Are your hens fed soy-free feed?

Yes — our chickens are fed a non-GMO, soy-free balanced poultry feed. We believe in clean nutrition for our birds, which translates directly to cleaner eggs for your family.

🦆 Duck Eggs

How much do duck eggs cost?

Duck eggs are $12 per dozen. They're larger than chicken eggs and prized by bakers and chefs for their rich flavor and higher fat content.

What makes duck eggs different from chicken eggs?

Duck eggs are noticeably larger, with a bigger yolk and higher fat and protein content. The whites are thicker, making them exceptional for baking — cakes rise higher, pastries are flakier, and flavor is richer. The shells are also thicker and harder than chicken eggs.

Do duck eggs taste different?

Yes — duck eggs have a richer, slightly more robust flavor than chicken eggs. Some describe them as creamier. The difference is most noticeable when eaten on their own (fried, scrambled) versus baked into a recipe.

Are duck eggs safe for people with chicken egg allergies?

Sometimes, but not always. Duck and chicken eggs share some proteins, so people with chicken egg allergies may or may not react to duck eggs. We always recommend consulting a doctor or allergist before trying duck eggs if you have a known egg allergy.

How should I store and care for duck eggs?

The same as chicken eggs — refrigerate after purchase and use within 4–6 weeks. Because of their thicker shells, they tend to stay fresh slightly longer than chicken eggs under the same conditions.

🌿 CSA Membership

What is a CSA?

CSA stands for Community Supported Agriculture. It's a membership model where you commit in advance — monthly or seasonally — to receive food directly from the farm. Your prepayment helps us plan responsibly, care for our animals properly, and raise food the right way without cutting corners.

What share options are available?

We offer three share types:

The Layer Share — Pasture-raised eggs only (weekly or bi-weekly)
The Broiler Share — Whole pasture-raised chicken, fresh or frozen
The Full Ohana Share — Eggs + chicken combined (our most popular option)

Contact us to discuss availability and current pricing for the 2026 season.

Why do I need to prepay?

Prepaying isn't about locking you in — it's about how small farms work best. When members commit in advance, it lets us plan feed, housing, and animal care properly. It means we raise based on real numbers, not guesses. It reduces waste and keeps a local, honest food system viable. Your commitment is what makes it possible to do this right.

When does the 2026 CSA season start?

Our CSA is new for the 2026 season. Contact us at info@ohanaminifarm.com or (530) 270-9262 for current enrollment dates and availability.

What if I need to skip a pickup?

Life happens. Give us as much notice as possible — ideally at least a few days — and we'll work with you. We ask for advance notice so we can plan our harvest and share distribution accordingly.

Can I cancel my CSA membership?

CSA cancellations are accepted one week prior to your next delivery or pickup date. We understand circumstances change and will always work with you fairly. All sales on food already delivered are final.

What does "shared understanding" mean for a CSA?

Farming involves seasons, weather, and living animals — not factories or guarantees. A CSA is a partnership: we commit to caring for your food with integrity, and you commit to the realities of small-farm life. If a batch is affected by weather or unexpected loss, CSA members are always the first to know and the first to be taken care of in the next batch. Transparency goes both ways.

🌾 Farm Practices

What do you feed your chickens?

Our chickens receive a non-GMO, soy-free, balanced poultry feed formulated to support steady growth, proper bone development, and healthy muscle formation. We adjust feeding by growth stage — starter, grower, and finisher — rather than pushing for unnatural speed.

What do you feed your ducks?

Our ducks receive a balanced waterfowl diet with clean, fresh water always available. Contact us for specifics on current duck feed if you have dietary concerns.

How do you prevent parasites and illness without chemicals?

We focus on prevention over reaction through four main practices:

Fresh Ground — Coops are cleaned regularly and birds are rotated to new foraging areas. This breaks parasite life cycles naturally.
Clean Bedding — Dry, clean bedding discourages parasite breeding and bacterial growth.
Balanced Diet — Proper nutrition supports strong immune systems and natural resilience.
Daily Health Checks — We visually inspect every bird daily. Sick or injured birds are removed immediately to protect flock health.

Are your birds truly pasture-raised?

Yes. Our birds are raised outdoors in secure pasture pens with protection from predators and weather. They have space to move naturally, forage, dustbathe, and behave like birds. We prioritize leg health, clean ground, and low stress over rapid growth or maximum density.
