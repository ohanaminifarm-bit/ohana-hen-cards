The Basics

What Is a CSA?

A CSA is a farm membership where you receive regular food while supporting how it's grown.

CSA stands for Community Supported Agriculture. It's a subscription-style model where members commit in advance — monthly or seasonally — to receive food directly from the farm.

This allows farmers to plan responsibly, care for animals well, and grow food the right way — not the fast way.

At Ohana, your membership means a direct relationship with the people raising your food. No middlemen. No mystery. Just honest food from a farm you can trust.

Questions

Common Questions

How does pickup work?

All shares are picked up directly at Ohana Mini Farm. You'll receive a notification when your share is ready along with pickup instructions.

What if I need to skip a pickup?

Life happens. Reach out ahead of time and we'll work with you. We ask for as much notice as possible so we can plan accordingly.

Is the chicken fresh or frozen?

Both options are available. Fresh chicken is available around processing dates. Frozen is vacuum sealed immediately after processing for maximum quality and can be held until your pickup.

Are hormones or antibiotics used?

No hormones — this is required by U.S. law for all poultry. We also focus on prevention rather than reaction, using clean ground, balanced nutrition, and daily health checks to keep our birds healthy without routine antibiotics.

What does "non-GMO" mean for the feed?

Our birds receive a balanced poultry feed that contains no genetically modified ingredients. This supports steady, natural growth — not the artificially accelerated kind.

Can I visit the farm?

We love connecting with our members. Reach out to arrange a farm visit — we want you to see exactly how your food is raised.

What if a batch runs short due to weather or loss?

Farming involves seasons and animals — not guarantees. We will always communicate transparently if a batch is affected. Members are first to know and first to be taken care of in the next batch.
